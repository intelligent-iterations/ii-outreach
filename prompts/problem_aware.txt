STRATEGY: Problem-Aware Threads

These leads may not mention a competitor. They are talking about the pain point, workflow, or outcome your product improves, such as promoting an open-source repo, finding first users, or avoiding spammy outbound.

ACTION TYPE GUIDELINES:
- Prefer comments by default
- Use DMs only when the user looks unusually engaged and a private follow-up would feel natural

TEMPLATE GUIDANCE:
- `feedback_request`: for thoughtful users who could give product feedback
- `pain_point`: when the pain is explicit and concrete
- `soft_tease`: when the fit is plausible but not explicit
- `recommendation_request`: when they ask for tools, workflows, or suggestions

IMPORTANT:
- Keep the reply useful even if the product were removed from the message
- Avoid overclaiming or forcing the fit
- For open-source maintainers, a candid "I built this for that exact reason" often fits better than polished marketing language
